Brain Stroke Detection

A brain stroke is a medical emergency where blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, leading to brain tissue damage. It can cause sudden numbness or weakness, confusion, difficulty speaking or understanding speech, and other symptoms. Prompt medical attention is crucial to minimize damage and improve outcomes.

Suggestions to Prevent Stroke

Regular Exercise

Engage in regular physical activity such as brisk walking, swimming, or cycling for at least 30 minutes most days of the week to improve blood circulation and reduce the risk of stroke.

Healthy Diet

Follow a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit saturated fats, trans fats, cholesterol, sodium, and added sugars for heart and vessel health.

Manage Blood Pressure

Manage high blood pressure through lifestyle changes like a healthy diet, regular exercise, stress management, and prescribed medications.

Quit Smoking

Stop smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke to reduce the risk of stroke. Smoking damages blood vessels and increases the likelihood of blood clots.

Limit Alcohol Consumption

If you consume alcohol, do so in moderation. Excessive alcohol consumption can raise blood pressure and increase the risk of stroke and other cardiovascular diseases.

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Maintain healthy weight with balanced diet and exercise. Excess weight links to high blood pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol, raising stroke risk.

Manage Diabetes

Control blood sugar levels via healthy diet, exercise, glucose monitoring, and prescribed meds. Follow healthcare provider's guidance for optimal management.

Get Quality Sleep

Prioritize quality sleep with regular schedule, cozy environment, and relaxation techniques. Poor sleep escalates high blood pressure and other stroke risks

Manage Stress

Manage stress with exercise, relaxation, hobbies, socializing, and support from loved ones or professionals. Chronic stress can elevate blood pressure and stroke risks.
